The Scribner House

The chain for Lot 9 begins on July 14, 1823 — the same day that Job Howes conveyed Lot 4 to Jared Bouton. The deed was the second of two executed that morning: Job Howes and Elizabeth his wife conveyed a second parcel to Jared Bouton for another hundred dollars (Liber B/106). Stephen C. Barnum took the acknowledgment — the same Judge of Common Pleas who acknowledged the Lot 4 deed. Job Howes conveyed two separate Sodom Road properties to the same buyer on the same day, distributing what appears to have been a contiguous Howes family holding along the south side of what would become Sodom Road.

The 1838 Double Conveyance

The Bouton estate executors conveyed Lot 9 on April 5, 1838 — the same day they conveyed Lot 4 — to a joint grantee arrangement that placed the property in the hands of Charles H. Todd and Jacob O. Howes simultaneously. Jacob O. Howes, purchasing adjacent land on the same afternoon that he acquired another deed from the same estate, was actively assembling Southeast Centre real estate a decade before his lumber yard, a generation before his six confirmed Southeast Centre properties, and thirty-eight years before his death. Ebenezer Foster acknowledged the deed as Putnam County Judge — and was simultaneously listed as the boundary neighbor on the north and west of the very property being conveyed.

The Scribner Connection

The property description from an 1848 deed was carried verbatim for 109 years, appearing without modification in the 1957 deed — a remarkable example of the tenacity of early nineteenth century legal language in small-community property records. When Ursula Scribner purchased the property in 1866 in her own name — an unusual practice for the era — she paid $1,050. John Scribner, almost certainly her husband, appears as the grantor of the Lot 8 founding deed. The Scribner family thus connected both the Paddock House and the Scribner House chains in the 1845–1866 period.

The well-sharing easement established in the 1857 Lot 8 deed — shared water access with 104 Sodom Road — appears in every subsequent deed in both chains, a practical arrangement whose legal trace outlasted both households' original residents.

The Penny Family Thread

After the Scribners, an inheritance gap preceded Anna Hughes of Westchester, then Harry E. and Archibald Penny in 1948, Harry and Edith Penny in 1957, Comstock in 1971, and E. Snyder, the current owner. The Penny family thread — Philip S. Penny, Alice Penny Yale's father; Melissa Crosby Penny, Alice's aunt; Harry E. Penny appearing at both Lot 6 (96–98 Sodom Road) and Lot 9 — runs through multiple Southeast Centre properties simultaneously. These were not coincidental appearances; they reflect a family with deep and multi-generational investments in the neighborhood.
