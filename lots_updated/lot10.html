Church to School to Home

The structure at 112 Sodom Road has been, in sequence, a Presbyterian church, a public schoolhouse, and a private residence. Six independent primary sources document this transformation, making it the best-documented property conversion in the research area.

H.H. Roberts established the founding date in his 1876 centennial address: the Southeast Centre Presbyterian Church was "organized June 14th, 1853; built in 1853-4, dedicated in June, 1854." The church used here is the Presbyterian Church of South East Centre — not "Central Presbyterian Society," which name only appears from 1886 onward.

The $104 Sale and Baxter's Pattern

The church sold in 1891 to John R. Yale and James D. Baxter for $104 (Liber 73/297) — George Hine serving as the church trustee authorizing the sale. The same Baxter who had appraised and then purchased the parsonage (Lot 7) in 1889 now purchased the church building itself, continuing his systematic acquisition of distressed Southeast Centre properties while serving as Town Assessor. Baxter conveyed to School District No. 6 for $300. The church building — with its clerestory windows visible in the 1900 school photograph in the published Town of Southeast history — became the district school.

The Same-Day Double Recording

On September 10, 1901, Frederic S. Barnum orchestrated a same-day double recording: the school district's deed was recorded at ten in the morning; a Yale-to-Baxter deed was recorded at noon. Barnum's orchestration of same-day recordings to accomplish particular legal results is a pattern visible across multiple Southeast Centre instruments. The June 28, 1901 article in the Brewster Standard confirmed his role as the neighborhood's deed custodian: "All the papers were looked up in his safe." That same article identified John R. Yale as holding the warrantee deed to the Sodom church site — given by the original trustees — with the land's legal status having been disputed until Barnum produced it from his safe.

School Consolidation and Residential Conversion

The school consolidated in 1927. An auction notice in the Brewster Standard of June 1927 offered the "Sodom School Property" for sale with buildings included. Nellie W. Beal purchased for $3,425; P.F. Beal signed as school board president — presumably her husband, authorizing the sale to his own wife.

On January 11, 1929, the Brewster Standard reported that J.J. Donohue, "contractor and builder of New York City," had purchased the "former Sodom school house" and "expects to make the place his summer residence." The sale was brokered by real estate agent L.F. Schneider. A bus route notice of June 15, 1944 — "past former Sodom school house, now Donohue" — confirms the Donohue family in residence, the building standing, and the conversion complete.

Donohue to Caggiano, Caggiano to Fred Somma in 1956. Eleanor Somma died intestate on February 13, 1974 — "late of Sodom Road" — and her estate sold to Phillips in 1976 for $32,000. The clerestory windows that appear in the 1900 school photograph (page 35 of the published Town of Southeast history) are still visible today. The church that the Howes family's generation dedicated in June 1854, the school that their grandchildren attended, and the home that the Donohues renovated are the same building.
