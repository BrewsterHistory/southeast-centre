W.H. Crosby — Howes Street

W.H. Crosby is labeled on the 1867 Beers map at the junction of Howes Street and Foster Avenue, between J.E. Reed and Judge Foster (Ebenezer Foster). The "W.H." initial combination appears in the Crosby genealogical record for this generation, and the occupant is almost certainly William H. Crosby.

The Most Numerous Family

The Crosby family was — in the words of Haight's 1912 history — "the most numerous family in former times" in Southeast Centre. Three brothers settled here in 1756: Thomas, Daniel, and Joshua Crosby. The family name runs through the deed records of nearly every lot in the neighborhood across two centuries: Esther A. Crosby in the Lot 4 boundary descriptions; Seth S. Crosby (labeled "S.O. Crosby" on the Beers map due to an engraver's error) on the Main Street cluster; Bette O. Crosby as executor of the Jacob O. Howes estate; Martha M. Crosby as a Map 799 Double Reservoir I claimant (Parcel 62½, 0.717 acres); Louise Crosby O'Brien as devisee of the Wells estate in 1952.

Martha M. Crosby — almost certainly connected to this same household — received $4,804 in the July 1890 watershed awards for 12 of 80 acres and a barn. Her Map 799 claimant parcel (62½) sits in the same cluster as the Melissa Birch farmhouse parcels, confirming that the Crosby and Howes-Birch properties were neighboring households in 1867 and neighboring watershed parcels in 1890.

No Deed Research Completed

No deed chain has yet been established specifically for this parcel. The property was lost to the watershed era. Future research should begin with the grantor/grantee index under Crosby in the Putnam County Clerk records, cross-referenced against Map 799 Parcel 62½ for Martha M. Crosby.
