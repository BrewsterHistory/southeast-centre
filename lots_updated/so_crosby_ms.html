S.O. Crosby — Main Street

The "S.O. Crosby" label on the 1867 Beers map — appearing twice, once on the north side and once on the south side of Main Street — has been confirmed as Seth S. Crosby. The "O" is a map engraver's misreading of the handwritten "S." in "S.S. Crosby." This was confirmed through a correction certificate at Liber G/105, which explicitly corrects the name.

The Seth S. Crosby chain on the Main Street parcels has been traced through four transactions back to 1830. The root title flows from the David Reed homestead through Seymour Allen: Allen conveyed to Seth S. Crosby in 1830 (Liber G/97). Subsequent instruments confirmed in the chain include the Marvin→Crosby conveyance (Liber I/542), the Wooster→Crosby conveyance (Liber I/543), and the Wyatt assignees→Crosby conveyance (Liber X/32). Seth S. Crosby was active in Southeast Centre real estate through the 1850s.

The Crosby-Reed-Crosby Loop

Seth S. Crosby conveyed his Main Street parcel to Shedrick Reed (the "S. Reed" boot and shoe manufacturer on the adjacent parcel) in 1852 and 1856 — placing the two neighbors in a direct commercial and real estate relationship. Reed's will, probated in 1884, ultimately returned the parcel to Martha Crosby, completing a documented Crosby→Reed→Crosby loop across three decades. The family relationships that defined Southeast Centre's social geography are visible in the deed record.

This parcel was taken by the City of New York in the watershed proceedings. It now lies within the watershed boundary.
