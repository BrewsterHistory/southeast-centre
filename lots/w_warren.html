W. Warren — Shoemaker

William Warren is labeled on the 1867 Beers map east of the Presbyterian Church on Church Street (Sodom Road), on the western portion of the Yale Tract. The 1880 federal census identifies him as a shoemaker, born approximately 1817 — a resident of Carmel, New York in 1846 who arrived at Southeast Centre between 1846 and 1864. He held a separate half-acre house lot here, mortgaged to Jacob O. Howes in 1864.

His widow Abagail Warren first advertised the property in the Brewster Standard of February 25, 1887 (“The undersigned offers for sale or to rent at reasonable terms her place in Southeast Center. Mrs. Wm. Warren”) and was still advertising it on July 22, 1887 — the same issue that carried the formal watershed appraisal notice. The five-month gap between the two ads documents that she was trying to sell well before the watershed proceedings became formal: William Warren had died sometime before February 1887, and his widow was attempting to liquidate the property as the City of New York's intentions became increasingly visible. She ultimately conveyed to Vilettee Birch (the Howes estate executrix) in 1895 for $400 (Liber 70/303). Vilettee Howes Birch — Jacob O. Howes's daughter — is recorded variously across deeds and newspapers as Vilette, Violette, or Violetta; her gravestone at Milltown Rural Cemetery records her name as Vilettee, the spelling used in this research.

The Chair Factory Connection

The Warren house site is confirmed by three converging lines: the 1867 Beers map footprint, the deed metes and bounds, and the notation "Carpenters Shop now or formerly" in the deed description — which corresponds to the Wright & Co. Chair Factory confirmed demolished in December 1889 by the Brewster Standard. The Warren house sat immediately adjacent to the factory site. The current structure at 116 Sodom Road is a mid-twentieth-century ranch built during the Killarney era. The site is the Warren site; the building is not.
