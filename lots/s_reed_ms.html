S. Reed — Boot & Shoe Manufacturer

S. Reed is labeled on the 1867 Beers map along Main Street, north side, in the cluster now under active deed research (Tax Map Section 57.17). The Beers Business Directory confirms: "S. Reed — Manuf. of Boots & Shoes." Shedrick Reed is the confirmed identity, established through the Crosby→Reed conveyances of 1852 and 1856 (Libers Y/238 and 30/326) and Reed's will probated in 1884 (to Hannah A. Reed, Liber 63/491).

The boot and shoe manufacturing operation represents the kind of small-scale rural manufacturing that characterized Southeast Centre at its commercial height. Shedrick Reed received his parcel from Seth S. Crosby — the neighboring "S.O. Crosby" on the Beers map — placing these two manufacturers in a direct neighbor and grantor-grantee relationship. After Reed's death, the parcel returned to Martha Crosby through his will, completing the documented Crosby-Reed-Crosby loop.

The parcel was taken by the City of New York in the watershed proceedings and now lies within the watershed boundary.
