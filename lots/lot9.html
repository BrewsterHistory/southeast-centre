There is a remarkable legal detail buried in this chain: the property description Ursula Scribner received when she bought this house in 1866 was copied word for word into every deed that followed — unchanged — for the next 91 years, until 1957. The same landmarks, the same language, the same metes and bounds. Whatever else changed in the neighborhood over nine decades, the description of this lot held perfectly still.

The chain begins on the same afternoon as the chain for 88 Sodom Road, two properties west. Job Howes conveyed both lots to Jared Bouton on July 14, 1823 — the same grantor, the same grantee, the same session. The two properties were born together and have been separately owned ever since.

Lorenzo D. Bradley bought this parcel in 1848, moved to New Fairfield, Connecticut, and left the deed unrecorded for twelve years. When he finally filed in March 1860, he recorded both his old acquisition deed and his fresh sale deed to Granville Hodge on the same afternoon — twelve years of paperwork cleared in a single visit to the county clerk's office.

The well shared between this property and the Paddock House next door first appears in the 1848 deed. It ran, in one form or another, through every subsequent instrument for over a century — a practical fact of daily life encoded in the legal record.
