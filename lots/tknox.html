T. Knox — Main Street

The parcel labeled "T. Knox" on the 1867 Beers map sits on the south side of Main Street (the old Croton River Turnpike Road, now Sodom Road / Old Route 22), in the cluster of properties that escaped the watershed taking because they fronted on the road rather than extending into the reservoir area. The modern address is 62 Sodom Road, Tax Map Section 57-17-1-6.

The founding deed is Liber T/64 (March 13, 1846): John K. Wyatt and Julia A. Wyatt conveyed a half-acre house lot to Thaddeus R. Knox for $200. N.D. Barnum, County Clerk, recorded the deed — the earliest confirmed Barnum family appearance in Southeast Centre deed recording, predating Frederic S. Barnum's later appearances as neighborhood attorney by approximately forty years. The root title for the entire Main Street cluster is the homestead of David Reed, distributed through the Wyatt insolvency estate in the 1840s and 1850s.

The Knox family held through Theodore Raymond Knox; Laura Taylor Knox as sole devisee conveyed to John L. Godfrey via two quitclaim deeds in 1916–17, both notarized by Frederic S. Barnum (Libers 112/441 and 114/597). The 62 Sodom Road structure appears in a hand-tinted postcard captioned "Main Street, Sodom, N.Y." (c.1900–1915) and survives in a 2019 Google Street View — one of the few Southeast Centre structures identifiable continuously from the pre-watershed era to the present through both photographic and deed evidence.

The Philipse Patent Connection

A 1974 deed in the chain explicitly reserves "mining and mineral rights in the heirs of Philip Philipse, if any." The Southeast Centre historic marker confirms the neighborhood sits within the former Philipse Patent. This reservation is a direct documentary link from this parcel to the colonial land grant era — running from a seventeenth-century patent through two and a half centuries of New York property law to a routine 1974 deed.
