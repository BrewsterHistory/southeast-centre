A. Burch — Burch & Beers, Fur Hat Manufacturers

A. Burch is labeled at the lower end of Main Street on the 1867 Beers map. The Beers Business Directory confirms the enterprise: "Burch & Beers — Manufacturers of Fur Hats." Two separate hat manufacturing operations in one small village — Burch & Beers here and the J.O. Howes Hat Factory at Sodom Corners — confirms that hat manufacturing was genuinely central to the village economy in the 1860s.

H.H. Roberts, in his 1876 centennial address, used the nickname "Hatesville" for Southeast Centre — a name that only makes sense if hat production was a defining feature of the neighborhood's commercial identity. The Burch & Beers operation at the southwestern end of Main Street was part of that industrial concentration.

No deed research has yet been completed on the Burch parcel. The property sits in the Main Street cluster (Tax Map Section 57.17) that was distributed through the John K. Wyatt insolvency estate in the 1840s and 1850s. Like most of Main Street, it was taken by the City of New York in the watershed proceedings.
