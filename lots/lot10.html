No property in Southeast Centre has lived more lives than this one. Church from 1854 to 1891. School from 1901 to 1927. Private home ever since. Three complete institutional identities on the same half-acre lot — and the bones of the 1901 schoolhouse are almost certainly still inside.

The church sold for $104 in 1891. That price — barely more than a month's wages for a skilled laborer at the time — captures exactly how far the neighborhood had fallen in the three years since reservoir construction began. The building was 37 years old and worth almost nothing to the congregation leaving it behind.

The 1901 school transaction is one of the most precisely engineered in the deed record. Frederic S. Barnum arranged a same-day double recording: the deed conveying the lot to School District No. 6 was filed at 10 a.m. The deed explaining how Emma Baxter had acquired it — from John R. Yale — was filed two hours later at noon. The school's title was established in the public record before the chain of title explaining it had even been recorded. Legal sequencing as an art form.

A 1944 school bus route notice tells drivers to pass "the former Sodom school house, now Donohue" — the family had been there since 1929, and the old schoolhouse was already a recognized landmark by name. The clerestory windows visible today match the 1900 photograph precisely. Same building, different life.
